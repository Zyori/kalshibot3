# Global Trading Principles — All Sports

## It's trading, not betting

- **Prediction market: you can ALWAYS sell before the event ends.** Every position is a
  tradeable price, not a ticket held to resolution.
- The question is never "will this outcome happen?" — it's **"where will this PRICE go, and
  when do I sell?"**
- You profit from the price moving your way, then exiting. You rarely need the event to finish.

## The edge

- **Buy when the price is cheaper than the game state deserves; sell when it's richer.**
  "Cheap/rich" = relative to what the game is about to do (conventional game logic), not vs an
  external sharp book.
- Watching momentum isn't the edge — everyone sees momentum. The edge is acting on the gap
  between what's happening and what the price has caught up to.

## Behavioral mispricings (where the public is wrong)

- **Fade overpriced fan favorites.** Popular teams/players attract sentiment money; their price
  inflates above fair. Fade when the premium is clear.
- **Favor "boring" outcomes the public avoids when underpriced** — draws, unders, low-scoring,
  defensive results. Fans bet for excitement; the unexciting side is often cheap.
- **React to key events — the swing is the trade.** A goal, score, red card, injury, or big
  play reprices the market fast and often overshoots. Trade the overshoot.

## Mechanics

- **Use limit orders to capture value on spikes and reversals.** When a fan-driven spike or an
  overreaction to an event dislocates the price, set a limit to get filled at the value level
  rather than chasing market.
- **Time decay:** a position drifts as the clock kills the outcomes working against it. Ride
  the drift, but exit before the high-variance window where it can reverse fast.

## Discipline (the rules that protect the bankroll)

- **Don't average down on a thesis the game state is refuting.** A losing position getting
  cheaper is not a reason to add — only add when the read gets *better*, not when it gets cheaper.
- **Open every position with a soft close target in mind** — plus the conditions/trends that
  would move that target. Not a hard stop; a planned exit that adjusts as the game tells you to.
